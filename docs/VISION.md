# GM Shield — Vision & Purpose

> *Version 1.0 — Project Foundation Document*

---

## The Problem

Tabletop RPGs are one of the most creative, social, and rewarding hobbies a person can have. And yet, they consistently put up barriers that keep people from ever sitting down to play.

### Barrier 1: The Book Problem

Every RPG system comes with a rulebook. Some are 80 pages. Others are 500. Most are somewhere in between, written in a style that assumes you already understand what a "dice pool" or a "difficulty level" means.

When someone new hears "let's play Star Wars" and then gets handed a 512-page PDF, two things happen: they feel overwhelmed, and then they feel embarrassed about feeling overwhelmed. So they either fake it at the table, or they never show up.

The official Quick Start products that exist for some systems are often still too long, too dense, or too dependent on a specific pre-written adventure to be useful as a standalone teaching tool.

### Barrier 2: The Language Problem

Many popular RPG systems — especially older and indie titles — were never translated into Portuguese. Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game (WEG), for example, has a massive international fanbase and has been in print (officially and as a fan work) for decades. But almost all of its reference material, supplements, and community resources exist only in English.

Brazilian players who don't read English fluently are effectively locked out of entire systems. They either struggle through the original text, rely on sparse community-translated fragments scattered across forums, or simply never play those games.

### Barrier 3: The Table Problem

Even for experienced GMs who know their system cold, running a session smoothly requires quick access to a large number of reference values: difficulty numbers, damage tables, condition effects, action economy rules, weapon stats, and more.

Physical GM screens solve this problem partially — but they are expensive, system-specific, and often contain more flavor art than actual useful content. And they don't work on a laptop or tablet.

A GM who has to stop the session to flip through a book for two minutes loses narrative momentum. The players notice. The magic breaks a little. It happens too often.

---

## The Solution: GM Shield

GM Shield addresses all three barriers with two complementary tools, built for every supported system.

### Quick Start Guides

Not an adventure module. Not a trimmed-down rulebook. A **learning document** — written for someone who has never played the system and wants to sit down and play within the next hour.

A GM Shield Quick Start teaches:

- What the system is and what makes it distinctive
- The core mechanic, explained simply, with examples
- Character creation, step by step
- The essential rules for a first session: tests, combat, special abilities
- A reference summary at the end

It does not include:
- Full skill lists
- Every optional rule
- Extensive lore or setting text
- Anything a player or GM can figure out at the table

The goal is confidence, not completeness. A person who reads a GM Shield Quick Start should be able to sit down and run or play a session without consulting the original rulebook.

Additionally, the Quick Start is **print-ready**. A clean, well-formatted PDF version can be downloaded or printed, giving people a physical reference they can bring to the table.

### Digital GM Screens

A purpose-built reference interface for GMs running sessions.

Every physical GM screen puts the most-referenced tables and rules in front of the GM during play. GM Shield does the same digitally — but without the limitations of a folded cardboard panel.

A GM Shield Digital GM Screen:

- Organizes all reference content into named, collapsible panels
- Loads fast and requires no internet connection after the first visit
- Works on any screen size, with special attention to laptop and tablet layouts
- Never requires vertical scrolling to find what you need
- Can be kept open in a browser tab during the entire session

## Audience

### Primary Audience

**The New Player**
Someone who wants to try tabletop RPGs but doesn't know where to start. They may have seen someone stream D&D, watched a Star Wars film, or heard about Ordem Paranormal from Brazilian content creators. They want to play, but they're intimidated.

**The New GM**
Someone who has played RPGs before — as a player — and wants to try running their first session. They need a concrete, practical reference, not a philosophical treatise on how to tell stories.

### Secondary Audience

**The Experienced GM (New System)**
A seasoned GM who wants to run a new system quickly, without reading an entire rulebook. They know how RPGs work; they just need to absorb the specifics of this new system efficiently.

**The Brazilian RPG Community**
Players and GMs who engage with RPG systems that have no Portuguese-language official material. GM Shield prioritizes these systems, creating original content in Portuguese that makes them accessible to an audience that has historically been underserved.

## Principles

**Clarity over completeness.**
It is better to teach five rules clearly than twenty rules poorly. The Quick Start is not a rulebook — it is a first step.

**Respect the original work.**
GM Shield does not reproduce copyrighted material. It creates original explanatory content inspired by, but independent from, the official rules. The goal is to bring players *to* the official books, not to replace them.

**Practicality over aesthetics.**
The GM Screen exists to be used, not admired. Every layout decision is evaluated by one question: can the GM find what they need in under five seconds?

**Accessibility for all devices.**
A GM may have a laptop, an iPad, a tablet, or a large monitor at the table. The interface must work well on all of them.

**Consistency through templates.**
Every system follows the same structural template. This makes the project maintainable, predictable for contributors, and familiar for returning users who switch between systems.

**Content-code separation.**
System content (rules, tables, examples) lives in JSON data files, not in HTML. This makes content easy to update without touching the codebase.

---

## What GM Shield Is Not

**It is not a virtual tabletop (VTT).**
GM Shield does not host character sheets, dice rollers, maps, or campaign management tools.

**It is not a piracy platform.**
GM Shield does not host, link to, or distribute official rulebook PDFs. All content is original.

**It is not a wiki.**
GM Shield is curated, not exhaustive. The value of the project comes from its editorial decisions — what to include, what to leave out, and how to explain things clearly.

**It is not affiliated with any publisher.**
GM Shield is an independent fan project. It is not endorsed by West End Games, Wizards of the Coast, Chaosium, Jambo, Free League, or any other publisher.

---

## Success Metrics

GM Shield will be considered successful if it achieves the following:

- A person who has never played Star Wars WEG REUP can read the Quick Start and run or play a session without consulting the original rulebook.
- A GM running any supported system can keep the Digital GM Screen open during a session and find any rule or table in under five seconds.
- The project structure is clear enough that a new contributor can add a system without help from the original author.
- At least one person in the Brazilian RPG community tries a system they would otherwise have skipped because of the language barrier.
